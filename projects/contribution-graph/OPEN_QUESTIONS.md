## Q6: What does onboarding deliver in the first 5 minutes?

### The Answer

**The hook is the map.** The user takes away a personalized working document that:
1. Names their "operating wavelength" (the feeling-state of intrinsic engagement)
2. Shows 3–5 concrete examples of voluntary action they've taken
3. Identifies 2–3 open questions about their strengths they hadn't asked before

### Why This Is Compelling (in 5 minutes)

The 5-phase conversation is designed to produce insight during the conversation itself — not at the end. The key mechanisms:

- **Phase 1 (Opening):** "What's something you did recently you didn't have to do?" — The user answers with a real story. They haven't prepared this. The act of remembering and articulating it is itself valuable.
- **Phase 2 (Orientation):** "What's most 'you' about the way you did it?" — Distinguishes outcome satisfaction from process satisfaction. People rarely think about *how* they work, not just *what* they produced.
- **Phase 3 (Evidence):** "What are you proud of that nobody recognized?" — Surfaces evidence of intrinsic motivation without external validation.
- **Phase 4 (Mirror):** Bot shows what emerged. User reacts. The map is NOT a personality type — it's a set of live questions about themselves.
- **Phase 5 (First Stretch):** "Here's a small thing you could try. Here's what we'd observe." — They leave with a concrete next step and a reason to return.

### The Specific Deliverable (Takeaway)

After 5 minutes, the user has:
- A named "operating wavelength" (their intrinsic reward state)
- A document they can screenshot and share
- A QR code linking to their live map (updates over time)
- One challenge invitation in their pocket

### Validation Required (Test 0.1)

Ask: "Would you screenshot this and send it to a friend? Why or why not?"
- ≥5/10 should say yes with a specific reason related to the content, not just "it's cool"
- If the answer is "I wouldn't share this" — the map isn't compelling enough

## Q7: What perk actually motivates the 16–25 demographic?

### The Research-Backed Answer

Based on the JA Latvia / Gen-E context (young entrepreneurs, ages 16–25, in transitional moments):

**Ranked by likely motivational pull:**

| Rank | Perk | Why It Works | Risk |
|------|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | **Verifiable contribution record** | Evidence that work mattered. "I did X, which enabled Y." This is what CVs and self-assessments can't capture. | Must be tied to a real org/outcome |
| 2 | **First access to opportunities** | Priority access to roles, events, and introductions they couldn't get otherwise | Needs real stakes — can't be fake scarcity |
| 3 | **Skills mapped to their specific gaps** | Not generic skill-building. "Based on your pattern, you haven't had a chance to develop X. Here's a way to try it." | Requires behavioral data to be meaningful |
| 4 | **Connection to people who did interesting things** | Network effect. "People like you (pattern) tend to end up doing X." | Requires a large enough user base |
| 5 | **Recognition from real organizations** | Non-monetary credential — "Recognized by [Million Candles / JCI Latvia / Gen-E]" | Needs brand trust from partner orgs |

**What does NOT work for this demographic:**
- Monetary rewards (conditions out intrinsic motivation)
- Badges and gamification (too abstract)
- "Career development" (too distant — they want immediate relevance)

### Recommended Perk Stack (Phase 0 Validation)

For Test 0.1 and Test 0.3:
- **Immediate:** Take-home map (free, instant, personal)
- **Short-term (1–4 weeks):** Partner organization access (Gen-E, JCI Latvia, Million Candles)
- **Medium-term (1–3 months):** Real opportunity matching (specific role on a real project)

### Validation Required (Test 0.1)

Ask: "What would make you come back on Day 7?"
- The answer should be concrete and achievable (not "a job" or "money")
- If ≥4/10 say something about access, connection, or evidence → perk stack is correct
- If ≥3/10 say "nothing" or "I wouldn't" → retention mechanic needs redesign
