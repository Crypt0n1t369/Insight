## Purpose

Validate whether real organizations have problems that distributed contributors could solve, and whether they'd pay for access to proven contributors.

**Hard pass criteria:**
- ≥3/5 express willingness to pay at some price
- ≥2/5 give a concrete budget range or "would pay €X for this"
- ≥1 problem is genuinely solvable by a distributed team (not requiring on-site presence or proprietary context)
- The objections reveal what's missing in the current concept

**Critical constraint:** Do NOT build anything. Do NOT show a demo. Describe the concept in plain language and listen. The goal is to find real problems, not to close a sale.

## The 1-Page Concept Summary (Leave or Send Before the Call)

> ## What I'm Building
>
> A platform that finds you the right person for a problem — not the most available, not the most expensive, but the one whose behavioral history suggests they'll be genuinely good at this specific thing.
>
> **The core bet:** How you actually perform under pressure, in ambiguity, and in collaboration is a more reliable predictor than a CV or an interview. And that data exists in how people behave, not just what they say.
>
> **How it works (simplified):**
> 1. Contributors join and do short structured "challenges" — real micro-tasks with clear outcomes
> 2. The platform builds a behavioral profile from how they perform, not just what they claim
> 3. When a client posts a problem, the platform matches them to contributors whose profile fits the problem type
> 4. Clients pay for access to matched contributors; contributors earn through demonstrated capability
>
> **What this is NOT:**
> - Not a freelance marketplace (Upwork for things that already exist)
> - Not a job board (you don't post a job and get CVs)
> - Not a prediction market or a contest
>
> **The problem I'm trying to solve:** Most organizations can't solve novel problems with their existing team (wrong skills, wrong bandwidth, groupthink). Hiring is too slow. Consultants are too expensive. Crowdsourcing is too noisy. What if you could access proven contributors — selected for fit, not just availability?
>
> **Stage:** Early research. Testing whether the problem is real before building anything.

## Conversation Guide (10-Minute Pitch + Probing)

### Opening (1 minute)

*"Thanks for making time. What I want to understand today is whether a problem I'm seeing is actually real for you — not to sell you anything, just to learn. I'll describe what I'm thinking, then ask you some questions. Feel free to push back on anything that sounds like it won't work."*

---

### Concept Description (3–4 minutes — keep it conversational)

*"Here's the picture: every organization has problems they can't solve with their current team — wrong skills, not enough bandwidth, or just fresh eyes needed. Hiring is slow. Consultants are expensive. Crowdsourcing produces too much noise.*
>
> *I'm working on a different approach. What if you could access a network of contributors — people who've been profiled through real micro-work, not just self-assessments — and get matched to the one who's specifically suited for your problem type?*
>
> *The key difference from existing models: we're profiling people by how they actually perform, not what they say they can do. So when we match you to someone, it's based on demonstrated behavior in similar challenges, not a resume or a proposal.*
>
> *Contributors earn a reputation through real work. Clients pay for proven fit."*

**Stop talking here. Move to questions.**

---

### Problem Diagnosis Questions (5–6 minutes)

Use these in order. Follow interesting threads. Don't rush.

**Q1 — Real problem check:**
*"Does that resonate with anything in your world? Is there a problem right now that you can't throw at your current team — either because they don't have the skills, or the bandwidth, or because you need an outside perspective?"*

> **Probe:** What specifically? How long has this been a problem? What have you tried?
> **Note:** If they say "not really" or "we have consultants for that" → soft pass, dig deeper on Q2

**Q2 — Hiring/team gap:**
*"When you need capability you don't have, what's your current approach? Freelancers? Agencies? Full hires? What works and what doesn't?"*

> **Probe:** What's the biggest frustration with each approach?
> **Note:** If they say "we just work nights/weekends" → significant signal. Understaffed + overloaded.

**Q3 — The specific question:**
*"If this platform existed today and had a proven track record — let's say contributors had been tested on similar challenges — would you actually post a problem here? Or would you still go to who you already know?"*

> **Probe:** What would make you trust the matching? What would make you hesitant?
> **Listen for:** "I would need to see..." / "I don't think..." / "This is different because..."

**Q4 — Price sensitivity (get specific):**
*"Let's say you had a problem you wanted help with. At what price does this become a no-brainer for you? At what price would you never consider it, regardless of quality?"*

> **Note:** Don't accept vague answers. Push: "Give me a number. Even a range is useful."
> **Probe:** €200? €2,000? % of project value? Subscription?
> **This is the most important number in the test.**

**Q5 — Attribution / IP concern:**
*"One concern people often have: if you post a problem here, what happens to the solution? Who owns the work?"*

> **Probe:** Does this stop you from using a platform like this? Is it about confidentiality? IP? Or is it secondary to finding the right person?

**Q6 — Objections (the real test):**
*"What's the version of this that wouldn't work for you? If you were going to tell a colleague 'don't bother with this,' what would your reason be?"*

> **This is the most important open question.** Let them talk. Don't defend. Note every objection verbatim.

---

### Close (1 minute)

*"This is exactly what I needed. One last thing — do you know anyone else who'd have a strong opinion on this? Even if it's 'this is a terrible idea' — I'd want to talk to them too."*

> **Get 1 referral. Thank them.**
