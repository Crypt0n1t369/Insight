# Commercial Solar Energy in Latvia
## A Comprehensive Guide for Large Energy Consumption Companies

---

## Executive Summary

Latvia presents a compelling opportunity for commercial solar installations, particularly for companies with significant energy consumption. The country offers a favorable combination of EU-supported financing mechanisms through ALTUM (the Latvian Development Financial Institution), improving grid infrastructure, and ambitious renewable energy targets. Key highlights:

- **Strong financial support**: ALTUM provides loans, guarantees, and grants for solar projects
- **EU funding available**: Recovery and Resilience Facility, Cohesion Fund programs
- **Net metering available**: Self-consumption with excess electricity compensation
- **Growing market**: Solar capacity increasing rapidly with supportive policy framework
- **Typical payback**: 6-10 years depending on system size and financing structure

This document provides detailed guidance for businesses considering commercial solar installations in Latvia.

---

## 1. Support and Incentives for Commercial Solar in Latvia

### 1.1 ALTUM Programs (Primary Government Support)

ALTUM is the state-owned development finance institution managing most renewable energy incentive programs in Latvia.

**Key Programs:**

| Program | Type | Target | Support Level |
|---------|------|--------|---------------|
| Energy Efficiency for Businesses | Loan + Grant | Commercial buildings | Up to 45% grant + favorable loans |
| Renewable Energy Program | Loan | Solar installations | Up to 70% financing |
| Climate Change Program | Guarantees | Green projects | Up to 80% guarantee |

**Contact:**  
- Website: https://www.altum.lv
- Email: info@altum.lv
- Phone: +371 67095400

**Key Features:**
- Low-interest loans (typically 1-5% depending on program)
- Loan terms up to 20 years
- Combined financing (grants + loans) often available
- Technical assistance for project preparation

### 1.2 EU Funding Programs

**Recovery and Resilience Facility (RRF)**
- Latvia allocated significant funds for green transition
- Priority for solar installations combined with energy storage
- Application through ALTUM

**Cohesion Fund 2021-2027**
- Available for renewable energy projects
- Focus on reducing carbon emissions
- Large project thresholds apply

**European Agricultural Fund for Rural Development**
- For agricultural businesses with solar needs
- Rural development programs

### 1.3 Tax Incentives

**VAT:**
- Standard VAT rate: 21%
- Reduced VAT of 12% available for residential energy-efficient improvements (may extend to certain commercial projects)
- VAT can be recovered on solar equipment for business use

**Corporate Tax:**
- No special corporate tax breaks specifically for solar
- Standard corporate tax: 20% (on distributed profits)
- Tax depreciation: Straight-line over useful life (typically 5-7 years for solar equipment)

**Real Estate Tax:**
- Solar installations may be exempt from real estate tax in some municipalities
- Varies by local government

### 1.4 Net Metering / Self-Consumption Scheme

Latvia operates a **net metering system** for small and medium installations:

- **Up to 500 kW**: Full net metering, excess credited at retail electricity rates
- **Above 500 kW**: Market-based arrangements required
- Self-consumption is economically favorable due to high retail electricity prices
- Excess electricity can be sold to the grid

**Current Electricity Prices (2024):**
- Business electricity: €0.15-0.25/kWh (varies by consumption level)
- Grid fees: ~€0.03-0.05/kWh
- This makes self-consumption highly valuable

### 1.5 Green Energy Certificates

Latvia participates in the **Guarantees of Origin (GO)** system:

- Guarantees of Origin available for renewable electricity
- Can be sold separately from electricity
- Current market value: €1-3/MWh (varies)
- Useful for companies with sustainability reporting requirements

## 2. Limitations and Restrictions

### 2.1 Capacity Limits for Subsidies

| Category | Capacity Limit | Support Type |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Net metering | Up to 500 kW | Full retail credit |
| ALTUM small program | Up to 1 MW | Grants + loans |
| ALTUM large program | Above 1 MW | Loans + guarantees |
| Feed-in support | Varies | Competitive auctions |

### 2.2 Grid Connection Constraints

**Grid Operator:** AS "Augstsprieguma tikls" (AST) - transmission system operator

**Key Considerations:**
- Grid capacity varies significantly by location
- Urban areas (Riga, Daugavpils) generally have better grid access
- Rural areas may require grid upgrades at developer's cost
- Connection queues can be lengthy in popular areas
- Typical grid upgrade timeline: 6-24 months for large installations

**Grid Connection Process:**
1. Submit connection request to local distribution operator (Sadales tikls)
2. Technical assessment and offer (typically 30-60 days)
3. Grid upgrade if required (additional cost and time)
4. Installation and final connection

### 2.3 Land and Roof Ownership Requirements

**Roof-Mounted Systems:**
- Must have ownership or long-term lease (minimum 10+ years recommended)
- Building permits required from local municipality
- Structural assessment may be required for large systems

**Ground-Mounted Systems:**
- Land ownership or long-term lease required (typically 20+ years)
- Agricultural land may have restrictions
- Zoning/permitting requirements vary by municipality

### 2.4 Permitting and Zoning

**Required Permits:**
1. Building permit (from local municipality)
2. Grid connection agreement (from distribution system operator)
3. Environmental permit (for larger installations, >1 MW)
4. Construction permit (for structural elements)

**Timeline:**
- Building permit: 1-3 months
- Grid connection: 3-12 months
- Total process: 6-18 months for large projects

### 2.5 Local Content Requirements

- **No explicit local content requirements** for solar installations
- EU single market: Equipment can be sourced from any EU member state
- However, using local installers may facilitate permitting

## 3. Key Considerations for Large Commercial Installations

### 3.1 Payback Period Expectations

**Typical Payback Periods (Latvia):**

| System Size | Without Subsidy | With ALTUM Grant |
|-------------|------------------|------------------|
| 100-500 kW | 7-9 years | 5-7 years |
| 500 kW - 1 MW | 6-8 years | 4-6 years |
| 1-5 MW | 6-8 years | 5-6 years |

**Key Factors Affecting Payback:**
- Electricity prices (higher = shorter payback)
- Self-consumption rate (higher = shorter payback)
- System cost per kW
- Available subsidies/grants
- Grid upgrade costs

### 3.2 Grid Upgrade Costs

**Typical Costs:**
- Small connection (<100 kW): €5,000-15,000
- Medium connection (100-500 kW): €15,000-50,000
- Large connection (>500 kW): €50,000-200,000+

These costs can be significant and should be factored into project economics. AST may require security deposits during grid studies.

### 3.3 Energy Storage Considerations

**Battery Storage:**
- Costs decreasing (€300-500/kWh for commercial systems)
- Can maximize self-consumption rate
- Useful for peak shaving and grid services
- ALTUM programs increasingly support storage

**Recommended Approach:**
- Prioritize solar + storage for maximum self-consumption
- Consider storage if grid connection is constrained
- Storage can provide grid services revenue opportunities

### 3.4 PPA (Power Purchase Agreement) Options

**PPA Market in Latvia:**
- Developing market with limited traditional PPAs
- Some corporate PPAs available through energy traders
- Spot market prices can be volatile

**Options:**
1. **Self-consumption + net metering** (most common for commercial)
2. **Corporate PPA** with energy supplier (limited availability)
3. **Merchant model** - sell all electricity to grid (higher risk)

### 3.5 Leasing vs. Owned Systems

**Owned System:**
- Full benefits of incentives and depreciation
- Requires capital investment
- Full control over operations
- Recommended for companies with capital access

**Leasing/Power Purchase:**
- No upfront capital required
- Operational expenses (OPEX) vs. capital expenses (CAPEX)
- Limited access to tax benefits
- Available through third-party developers and ALTUM lease programs

### 3.6 Maintenance and Monitoring

**Annual Maintenance Costs:** €10-20/kW
- Panel cleaning (1-2x/year)
- Inverter maintenance/replacement (every 10-15 years)
- Vegetation management (for ground-mounted)

**Monitoring:**
- Remote monitoring standard with modern inverters
- Real-time performance tracking recommended
- Performance ratio monitoring for O&M optimization

### 3.7 Weather and Solar Irradiance in Latvia

**Solar Resource:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Annual GHI | 950-1,050 kWh/m² |
| Peak Sun Hours | 2.7-3.0 hours/day (annual average) |
| Best Month | June (~5.5 hours/day) |
| Worst Month | December (~0.5 hours/day) |

**Climate Considerations:**
- Long summer days (up to 17 hours of daylight in June)
- Snow can benefit panels (reflectivity increases output in winter)
- Lower temperatures improve panel efficiency
- Cloudy days reduce output but panels still generate

**Production Estimates:**
- Southern Latvia: ~950-1,000 kWh/kWp/year
- Northern Latvia: ~900-950 kWh/kWp/year

## 4. Specific to Latvia

### 4.1 Latvian Energy Market Structure

**Key Players:**
- **Transmission:** AS "Augstsprieguma tikls" (AST)
- **Distribution:** Sadales tikls (former regional companies)
- **Retail:** Multiple suppliers (latvian energy, enefit, etc.)
- **Balancing:** AST

**Electricity Prices (2024):**
- Industrial: €0.12-0.18/kWh
- Commercial: €0.15-0.25/kWh
- Households: €0.20-0.30/kWh
- Plus grid fees and taxes

### 4.2 AST (Grid Operator) Requirements

**Connection Process:**
1. Application to Sadales tikls (distribution) or AST (transmission > 110kV)
2. Technical conditions within 30-60 days
3. Grid study for large installations
4. Connection agreement and technical specifications

**Key Requirements:**
- Technical standards compliance (LVS standards)
- Metering equipment (required by grid operator)
- Protection systems for large installations
- Remote monitoring capabilities for >500 kW

### 4.3 Latvian Renewable Energy Targets

**EU Targets:**
- 2030: 50% renewable energy share (Latvia's target)
- National Energy and Climate Plan (NECP) 2021-2030

**Progress:**
- 2023: ~43% renewable energy (mostly hydro and wind)
- Solar growing rapidly but still small base (~100 MW installed)
- Target trajectory calls for significant solar expansion

### 4.4 Relevant Laws and Regulations

**Key Legislation:**
- **Energy Law (Enerģētikas likums)** - Framework for energy sector
- **Electricity Market Law (Elektroenerģijas tirgus likums)** - Market regulations
- **Renewable Energy Law (Atjaunojamās enerģijas likums)** - Support mechanisms
- **Building Law (Būvniecības likums)** - Permitting
- **Spatial Planning Law (Teritorijas plānošanas likums)** - Zoning

**Regulatory Authority:**
- Public Utilities Commission (SPK)
- Ministry of Economy

### 4.5 Available Consultants and Installers

**Major Solar Installers in Latvia:**
- **Solaris Energy** - Large-scale commercial installations
- **Eco Solar Latvia** - Commercial and industrial
- **Saules paneļi** - Residential and commercial
- **Baltic Solar** - International developer with Latvian operations

**Engineering/Consulting:**
- **Latvian Energy Consultants** - Project development
- **Enerģijas audits** - Feasibility studies
- Major audit firms (Deloitte, PwC, KPMG) have sustainability practices

**Recommended Approach:**
- Request quotes from 3-5 installers
- Verify licenses and past projects
- Check references from similar-sized installations

## 5. Recommended Next Steps

### Phase 1: Assessment (1-2 months)

1. **Energy Audit**
   - Analyze current electricity consumption patterns
   - Identify peak demand periods
   - Calculate potential solar offset

2. **Site Assessment**
   - Roof/land availability and condition
   - Structural integrity (for roof-mounted)
   - Shading analysis
   - Grid connection capacity

3. **Preliminary Design**
   - System size optimization
   - Initial layout and equipment selection
   - Cost estimates

### Phase 2: Financing & Permits (2-4 months)

1. **Financing Application**
   - Apply to ALTUM programs
   - Secure bank financing if needed
   - Finalize PPA or self-consumption model

2. **Permitting**
   - Building permit application
   - Grid connection application
   - Environmental permits (if required)

### Phase 3: Implementation (2-6 months)

1. **Procurement**
   - Equipment selection and ordering
   - Installer procurement

2. **Construction**
   - Installation
   - Grid connection
   - Commissioning

3. **Operations**
   - Monitoring setup
   - Maintenance contracts
   - Performance optimization

## 6. Summary: Key Decision Factors

| Factor | Consideration | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **System Size** | 500 kW - 5 MW optimal for most businesses | Size for 30-70% of consumption |
| **Financing** | ALTUM programs reduce cost of capital | Apply for combined loan + grant |
| **Self-Consumption** | Maximize for best economics | Target 70%+ self-consumption |
| **Grid Connection** | Early application critical | Start process immediately |
| **Storage** | Consider for peak shaving | Evaluate ROI for your use case |
| **Installer** | Local experience matters | Get 3+ competitive bids |
| **Timeline** | 6-18 months typical | Plan 12+ months ahead |
