# Youth Online Communities Research

## Key Findings

### What Attracts At-Risk Youth
1. **Escape** - Different identity, no judgment
2. **Agency** - Real impact, not token
3. **Community** - Belonging without explanation
4. **Skills** - Learn real things
5. **Money** - Path to income

### What Drives Them Away
1. **Bureaucracy** - Forms, waiting
2. **Judgment** - Being "helped"
3. **Tokenism** - "We're so proud of you"
4. **Institutions** - Everything feels institutional
5. **Paternalism** - Being told what's good

---

## Platform Behaviors That Work

### Discord
- No application
- Immediate community
- Voice chat = real connection
- Roles = identity

### Roblox  
- Create to earn
- No "volunteer" - real economy
- Safety without surveillance

### Reddit
- Pseudonym = identity
- Karma = credibility (visible)
- Subreddits = branches

---

## Design Principles for Credo

1. **No application** - Just wallet + start
2. **No "youth program"** - Just "community"
3. **Real contribution** - Not "junior" tasks
4. **Skills first** - What CAN you do?
5. **Mentors opt-in** - Elders help, not assigned
6. **No background** - Never ask "what did you do"
