# Youth Engagement: Special Research
## Reaching "Youth with Bad Backgrounds"

**Focus from Audio:** "youth with some bad background and they have met a lot of free time and they like to get in trouble and find an opportunity for them to feel as a meaningful contribution to society, showing them their strengths and ability to change things"

---

## Understanding This Population

### Who Are They?
- Ages roughly 16-25
- Often system-involved (juvenile justice, foster care, expelled, dropouts)
- High free time, unstructured
- Looking for identity, belonging, respect
- Can be smart but not "academic"
- Risk-taking orientation
- Often cynical of institutions

### What They Need
1. **Safety** - No judgment, no record, no background check
2. **Identity** - Be known for what they DO, not what they DID
3. **Agency** - Real impact, not token participation
4. **Belonging** - Community that accepts them
5. **Respect** - Treated as capable, not project
6. **Progress** - Visible growth, achievements
7. **Money** (eventually) - Path to legitimate income

### What Drives Them Away
- Bureaucracy
- Requirements (ID, email, phone)
- Being treated as "at-risk"
- Token "volunteer" positions
- Paternalistic programs
- Long wait times to contribution
- Fancy academic language

---

## Design Implications for Credo

### Entry Must Be
- [ ] Wallet-only (no email, no phone)
- [ ] Instant (no application)
- [ ] Anonymous (choose any name)
- [ ] Free (no cost ever for basics)
- [ ] Mobile-first (likely phone primary device)

### Onboarding Must
- [ ] Not ask "what's your background"
- [ ] Not ask "why are you here"
- [ ] Show contribution opportunities immediately
- [ ] Match to skills/interests quickly
- [ ] Connect with mentor within first session

### Contribution Path
- [ ] Low barrier first tasks (easy wins)
- [ ] Skill-based matching
- [ ] No "junior" or "newcomer" labels
- [ ] Fast credibility building
- [ ] Visible progress
- [ ] Badges for achievement
- [ ] Path to paid opportunities

### Community Must
- [ ] Not judge past
- [ ] Focus on what you do now
- [ ] Have clear norms (no tolerance for discrimination)
- [ ] Provide escalation path for issues
- [ ] Allow anonymity

## Platforms That Successfully Engage This Population

### Discord
- Low barrier (just make account)
- Community-based
- Voice chat (important for some)
- Role-based contribution

### Roblox
- Create/games, not just consume
- Economy that can become real income
- Youth-run communities
- Low judgment

### Twitch
- Streaming accessible
- Community building
- Potential income
- Identity experimentation

### Reddit
- Pseudonymous
- Niche communities
- Karma = credibility
- Self-moderation

### What Credo Can Learn
- Speed to contribution
- Identity flexibility
- Community belonging
- Potential real value

## Specific Credo Features

### "First Contribution" System
- Curated "easy first task" list
- Real contribution (not busywork)
- Quick credibility boost
- Mentor introduction

### "Skill Finder" Bot
- Chat with AI
- Finds what you're good at
- Matches to branches
- Recommends path

### "Growth Path" Visualization
- See where you can go
- Multiple paths
- Choose your adventure
- Visible next steps

### "Badge System" 
- Achievements for contribution
- Unique badges (not generic)
- Portfolio-building
- Exportable

### "Mentor Match"
- Opt-in mentors
- Structured check-ins
- Both benefit
- Graduated independence

### "Opportunity Board"
- Real paid opportunities
- Credibility-based access
- No middleman
- Direct connection
