# Sortition: Random Selection Governance

## What Is Sortition?
Governance by random selection (like jury duty). Ancient Athens used it.

## Traditional Applications

### Athens
- 500-600 citizens selected randomly per year
- No elections for most positions
- Prevents corruption and elitism

### Modern Experiments
- British Columbia's Citizens' Assembly
- Ireland's constitutional conventions
- France's climate conventions
- California's citizen assemblies

## Could Credo Use It?

### The Idea
- Instead of credibility-weighted voting
- Random selection for decisions
- "Jury duty" for governance

### Pros
- Every member has equal chance
- Prevents whale capture
- Encourages participation
- Builds genuine representation

### Cons
- Random people may not be qualified
- Can be gamed (fake engagement)
- No "skin in the game"
- Hard to implement well

## Hybrid Approach for Credo

**Recommended: Quadratic + Sortition**

```
1. Proposals need minimum credibility to create
2. Voting uses quadratic formula
3. Random "jury" of 5-10 randomly selected TRUSTED+ members
   makes final decision on contentious issues
4. Rotation: new jury each quarter
```

### Why This Works
- Filters spam (credibility threshold)
- Prevents whale capture (quadratic)
- Ensures competence (jury must be TRUSTED+)
- Adds randomness (prevents capture)
