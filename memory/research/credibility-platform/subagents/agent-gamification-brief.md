# Sub-Agent: Gamification & Behavioral Design Research
## Research Brief

**Agent ID:** subagent-gamification
**Mission:** Deep dive into gamification, behavioral psychology, and long-term engagement for Credo

---

## CORE CONCEPT

How do we create engagement that:
- Motivates meaningful contribution (not empty activity)
- Builds real credibility (not gaming)
- Keeps youth engaged (without addiction)
- Creates community (not competition)

---

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS

### Primary Questions
1. What gamification patterns create meaningful vs. performative activity?
2. How do we design for intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation?
3. What makes someone stay for years vs. churn after days?
4. How do we reward quality over quantity?

### Secondary Questions
5. What's the psychology behind "rep" and "karma" systems?
6. How do we prevent dark patterns (addiction, FOMO, etc)?
7. How can gamification serve the "youth with bad backgrounds" population?
8. What can we learn from tabletop RPG progression?

---

## AREAS TO INVESTIGATE

### A. Motivation Theory
- Self-Determination Theory (autonomy, competence, relatedness)
- Flow state (Csikszentmihalyi)
- Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation (Deci & Ryan)
- Research: "gamification motivation factors"

### B. Reputation System Psychology
- Why do people care about internet points?
- Social capital theory
- Signaling and status
- Research: "psychology of reputation systems"

### C. Progression Systems
- RPG levels (XP curves, tier systems)
- Achievement design (badges, unlocks)
- Seasonal vs. permanent progress
- Research: "player progression design patterns"

### D. Dark Patterns to Avoid
- Variable ratio reinforcement (slot machine mechanics)
- FOMO design
- Artificial scarcity
- Social pressure mechanics
- Research: "dark patterns in gamification"

### E. Collaboration vs. Competition
- Co-op mechanics in single-player games
- "Contribution" vs. "defeat" framing
- Guild dynamics (cooperate within, compete between)
- Research: "cooperative game design"

### F. Youth-Specific Gamification
- TikTok's engagement loop
- Roblox economy
- What attracts "troubled" youth?
- Achievement in adversity
- Research: "engagement design for at-risk youth"

### G. Long-Term Community Building
- Dinosaur clubs (USENET, old forums)
- What creates "veterans"?
- Mentorship mechanics
- Research: "sustainable online communities"
