# Sub-Agent: UX & Avatar System Research
## Research Brief

**Agent ID:** subagent-ux
**Mission:** Deep dive into UX paradigms and avatar systems for Credo

---

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS

### Primary Questions
1. What are the best UX patterns for complex branching/versioning interfaces?
2. How do successful platforms represent contribution/credibility visually?
3. What avatar systems work for pseudo-anonymous platforms?
4. How can we make "governance" feel accessible, not bureaucratic?

### Secondary Questions
5. What gamification UX patterns increase long-term engagement without addiction?
6. How do we visualize "branching" intuitively for non-technical users?
7. What onboarding flows work for "youth with bad backgrounds"?
8. How to represent "credibility tiers" without creating elite hierarchies?

---

## AREAS TO INVESTIGATE

### A. Branching Visualization (Non-Mainstream)
- Tree vs graph vs timeline representations
- Git visualization for non-developers (GitHub's graph, Linear's UI)
- Mental models of "forking" - what works mentally?
- Research: "spatial UI for version control"

### B. Avatar Systems
- Companion AI interfaces (Replika, Glow, Pi)
- Representation without identity (Bitmoji, Twitch emotes)
- Abstract avatars (ENS avatars, identicons)
- Research: "AI companions in productivity apps"

### C. Credibility Visualization
- Stack Exchange reputation display
- Reddit karma (hidden vs visible)
- DAO governance dashboards (MakerDAO, Compound)
- Research: "reputation systems UX"

### D. Anonymous Collaboration Patterns
- How 4chan sustains contribution without identity
- Reddit's voting dynamics
- Wikipedia's "signed vs anonymous" history
- Research: "anonymous contribution motivation"

### E. Youth-Oriented UX
- TikTok/Snapchat onboarding psychology
- "Safe failure
- Achievement systems" interfaces that build, not break
- Research: "youth engagement design patterns"

---

## ALIGNMENT TO CORE OBJECTIVES

✓ Attract talent/ideas - UX must feel welcoming, not gatekeeping
✓ Aggregate information - Visualize growth/accumulation
✓ Credibility system - Show progress clearly without shaming newcomers
✓ Iterative development - Make version history digestible
✓ Branching - Intuitive forking experience
✓ Unknown communities - Adaptable to diverse mental models
✓ Pseudo-anonymous - Identity optional, identity theft impossible
✓ Youth with bad backgrounds - Low friction, high reward, safe space
