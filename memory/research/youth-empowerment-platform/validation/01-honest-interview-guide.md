# Youth Empowerment Platform - HONEST Interview Guide

## ⚠️ WARNING: Challenge Your Assumptions

This is NOT a pitch. Your job is to find reasons to kill this idea.

---

## Interview Protocol

### Rules
1. **Never pitch** — Just present concept, observe reaction
2. **Probe negatives** — When they say something positive, ask "but what could go wrong?"
3. **Test specifics** — Vague interest means nothing. Get concrete.
4. **Find haters** — People who hate the idea are more valuable than fans.

---

## Screening Questions (2 min)

Before diving in, verify they're actually your target:

1. **Age?** (Need 16-25)
2. **What do you do now?** (Student? Working? Neither?)
3. **What apps do you use daily?** (This tells you their attention)
4. **Have you ever used a "youth program"?** (What was it? How did it feel?)

**RED FLAG:** If they say "I love LinkedIn" or "I have a perfect resume" — wrong demographic.

---

## Section 1: The Rejection Test (5 min)

### Don't say the pitch yet. Ask:

> "Imagine there's a new app for young people. It uses AI to help you find opportunities. Would you try it?"

**Let them answer freely. Then probe:**

- "Why/why not?"
- "What would make you NOT download it?"
- "Whatapps have you tried and stopped using? Why?"
- "What's wrong with existing apps?"

**Expected answer patterns:**
- ❌ "Sure I'll try it" (too polite)
- ❌ "Sounds cool" (meaningless)
- ✅ "I don't trust apps like that"
- ✅ "Another app? I have too many already"
- ✅ "Why would an AI help me?"
- ✅ "I don't need help finding opportunities"

**If they show no resistance → you're talking to the wrong person.**

---

## Section 2: The Concept Test (5 min)

### Only if they show initial interest, present concept:

> "Okay here's the idea: You create a private profile. No real name needed. An AI agent gets to know you — your skills, what you want, where you are in life. It matches you to real opportunities — jobs, projects, mentors. There's also a 'journey' thing where you track your growth."

### Then attack:

- "Does this sound useful? Why/why not?"
- "What's the worst thing about this idea?"
- "Who would actually use this? Who wouldn't?"
- "Is there anyone in your life who'd hate this?"
- "What does this remind you of? (Other apps, programs, etc)"

### Watch for:
- ❌ Eyes glazing over
- ✅ "Wait, how would it know what I want?"
- ✅ "An AI? Those are useless"
- ✅ "I don't want to be tracked"
- ✅ "This feels like a program"

---

## Section 3: The Privacy Paradox (3 min)

### Test if they really care about privacy:

> "This app encrypts all your data. Even the company can't see what you put in it. You'd have a 'vault' only you control."

### Probe:

- "Do you actually care if apps see your data?"
- "What happens if you forget your password? (Data gone)"
- "Would you rather: A) Free app that sees your data B) Paid app that doesn't"
- "Do you use Incognito mode? Why/why not?"
- "What's something you've never told anyone but would tell an AI?"

**Reality check:** Most youth share everything on Instagram. Do they actually want privacy?

---

## Section 4: The AI Test (3 min)

### Ask about AI relationships:

> "You'd have a personal AI that guides you. Like a chatbot that knows your goals, helps when you're stuck, suggests things."

### Probe:

- "Do you talk to AI chatbots? (ChatGPT, Character.AI, etc)"
- "What do you think of them?"
- "Would you trust an AI for this? Why/why not?"
- "What's the dumbest thing an AI has ever said to you?"
- "Would you rather talk to AI or a real person? Why?"

**RED FLAG:** If they say "AI is creepy" or "I only trust real humans" — your core feature is rejected.

---

## Section 5: The Journey Test (3 min)

### Present the "hero's journey":

> "The app has a 'journey' thing. You start at stage 1 and progress through stages as you grow. Like a video game. Each stage unlocks new opportunities."

### Probe:

- "Does this make sense?"
- "Does this feel... prescripted? Like you're being told what to do?"
- "Would this motivate you or annoy you?"
- "What would make it feel less like a program?"
- "Do you track progress in apps? (Duolingo streaks, etc)"

**Risk:** This could feel paternalistic — "we'll guide you." Youth may reject.

---

## Section 6: The Trust Test (3 min)

### Ask about trust:

> "To use the app, you'd set a passphrase. That encrypts everything. Even if the app got hacked, your data is safe. But if you lose the passphrase, we can't help you."

### Probe:

- "Have you ever lost a password and lost access to something important?"
- "Would you trust an app with this?"
- "What would make you trust this more?"
- "Would you rather: A) Less secure but easier B) More secure but you could lose everything"

---

## Section 7: The "Actually Use It" Test (5 min)

### Get specific:

> "Let's say you downloaded this. What's the first thing you'd do?"

**Wait for their answer. Then:**

- "Walk me through what you'd expect to happen."
- "How long would you spend in the app the first time?"
- "What would make you open it again tomorrow?"
- "What would make you delete it after one use?"

### Then:

> "Think of a friend who'd never use this app. Why wouldn't they?"

---

## Section 8: The Competition Test (3 min)

### Ask about existing solutions:

- "If you wanted to find a job or opportunity today, what would you do?"
- "What's wrong with LinkedIn?"
- "What about Indeed? ZipRecruiter?"
- "Do you trust 'youth programs'? (City Year, Year Up, etc)"
- "What works about Discord for finding communities?"

**Goal:** Understand why we'd be better. If they have no problem with existing solutions → we have no reason to exist.

---

## Closing (2 min)

### Ask:

- "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to use this? Why that number?"
- "What's the one thing that would make you actually try it?"
- "If this app existed, what would you name it?"
- "Who should we talk to next?"

### And most importantly:

> "You've been really honest. If we're building something dumb here, tell me. What are we missing?"
