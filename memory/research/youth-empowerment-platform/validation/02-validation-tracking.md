## Key Metrics to Track

### Per Interview:
- **Initial reaction** (1-10, genuine, not polite)
- **Objection count** (specific concerns raised)
- **Privacy score** (do they actually care?)
- **AI score** (do they trust AI?)
- **Journey score** (prescriptive or motivating?)
- **Concrete next step** (Y/N - will actually try)

### Aggregated:
- **Yes ratio** (should be 40-60% ideally)
- **Common objections** (top 5)
- **Feature requests** (top 5)
- **Demographic patterns** (which ages/backgrounds like it)

## What to Do If...

### Scenario: 8/10 say "I'd try it"
**Problem:** Too positive — may be talking to wrong people
**Action:** Recruit different demographic, harder screening

### Scenario: 8/10 say "I'd never use this"
**Problem:** Product rejected
**Action:** Identify specific objections, pivot or kill

### Scenario: Mixed reactions (4-6 yes)
**Problem:** None — this is good data
**Action:** Analyze differences between yes/no, iterate

### Scenario: Everyone mentions same objection
**Problem:** That's the real problem
**Action:** Fix that specific thing

### Scenario: No one can articulate why
**Problem:** Vague — not useful
**Action:** Better interview technique needed

## Honest Questions to Answer

After each interview, write 1 sentence:

1. **What did I learn that I didn't expect?**
2. **What objection keeps coming up?**
3. **Did any assumption get proven wrong?**
4. **Would I recommend continuing? Why/why not?**
5. **What's the single biggest risk this interview revealed?**

## Dead End Indicators

**STOP IMMEDIATELY if:**
- [ ] Can't find ANYONE in target demographic to talk to
- [ ] Target demographic doesn't exist (they all have jobs/college)
- [ ] Everyone says "it's a nice idea but..."
- [ ] No one can name a specific feature they'd use
- [ ] People confused about what the product actually is
