# Synthetic Characters Research

## Overview

Synthetic characters are AI-driven agents that can represent users in digital spaces, mediate conversations, and act as personalized interfaces between humans and complex systems.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Persona Synthesis
- Creating consistent AI representations of users
- Maintaining voice/tone consistency
- Value-aligned decision making

### 2. Egoless Representation
- User's synthetic character acts without ego
- Prioritizes truth over personal interests
- Represents user's values, not reactions

### 3. Mediation Capabilities
- Translating between different communication styles
- Summarizing positions across debates
- Finding common ground

## Research Questions

1. How do we ensure synthetic characters remain aligned with user values?
2. What boundaries should synthetic characters have?
3. How do we handle conflicts between user and synthetic character?
4. What are the legal implications of AI representation?

## Implementation Approaches

### Simple (Current)
- Rule-based responses
- Template-based summaries
- Keyword-triggered reactions

### Intermediate
- Fine-tuned models on user communication
- Context window with user history
- Personality embeddings

### Advanced
- Continual learning from user feedback
- Value alignment verification
- Multi-modal representation

## Connection to Credo

Synthetic characters are the interface layer for Credo:
- Users participate through their synthetic representatives
- Contributions are attributed through the character
- Reputation builds around the character's consistent behavior
