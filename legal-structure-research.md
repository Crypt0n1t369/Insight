# Legal Structures for Decentralized Platforms

## Options

### 1. Swiss Foundation
**Pros:**
- Neutral jurisdiction
- Strong privacy laws
- Well-understood
- Tax advantages

**Cons:**
- Expensive to set up
- Rigid structure
- Requires Swiss connections

**Best for:** Non-profits, mission-driven

---

### 2. US 501(c)(3)
**Pros:**
- Tax deductible donations
- Clear nonprofit status
- Grant-eligible

**Cons:**
- US jurisdiction
- More regulations
- Requires US presence

**Best for:** US-based teams

---

### 3. DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization)
**Pros:**
- No central authority
- Token-based governance
- Global

**Cons:**
- Unclear legal status
- Liability concerns
- Tax complexity

**Best for:** Fully decentralized projects

---

### 4. Swiss Association
**Pros:**
- Not-for-profit
- Flexible
- Swiss jurisdiction

**Cons:**
- Less known than foundation
- Still requires Swiss connection

---

### 5. Cayman Islands Foundation
**Pros:**
- Common for crypto
- Tax neutral
- Flexible

**Cons:**
- Reputation concerns
- Not for mission-driven

---

## Recommendation for Credo

**Phase 1:** Unincorporated (build first)
**Phase 2:** Swiss Foundation or US 501(c)(3)
**Phase 3:** Hybrid (DAO + Foundation)
